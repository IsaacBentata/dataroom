# Music Industry Partnerships

## Universal Music Group

We hold a full worldwide catalogue deal with UMG - the world's largest music company and the only superfan/social music platform to close one this year.

This isn't just a licensing deal. UMG has told us directly they want Equals to be a core part of their activation strategy. They see us as a way to speak directly to consumers - something streaming platforms don't let them do. They're also very interested in our first-party data on fan behaviour and engagement, which doesn't exist anywhere else.

We're now in ongoing conversations with their labels to activate artist releases on Equals, including exclusive pre-releases where fans hear new music on our platform before it hits streaming.

## Digital Vinyls

Digital Vinyls are branded digital downloads that users collect and pin to their profile's Vinyl Grid. They're collectibles that let fans express their music identity.

Labels love this for a very specific reason: download sales count toward chart positioning. If an artist drops a Digital Vinyl exclusively on Equals, those sales push them up the charts. This creates a straightforward incentive - labels drive fans to Equals for exclusive drops, fans get collectibles they actually want, and artists climb the charts. Everyone wins.

This is a net-new revenue stream for labels that doesn't cannibalise streaming income. It's additive.

## Why this matters

Streaming revenue has plateaued. Labels built their business on distribution, but Spotify and Apple Music now account for roughly 80% of their revenue and own the consumer relationship entirely. Labels have catalogue but no direct line to the fans who love their artists.

We're filling that gap. Equals is where labels can engage fans directly, drive commerce through Digital Vinyls and exclusives, and access data on how fans discover and connect through music. The UMG deal validates this. Closing Sony and Warner completes it.
