# Monetisation Strategy

## What we've proven

Between November 2025 and January 2026, we ran an extended A/B test to understand our monetisation ceiling. The results were strong - we hit a 4.5-5% paywall conversion rate across all installs, with significantly higher conversion on verified users. At our current MAU base, that would translate to $3M+ ARR if we turned it back on today.

But we also saw something important: aggressive monetisation was dampening network effects. Users who hit paywalls early were less likely to add friends, join chat rooms, and engage with the core social loops that drive retention. We were extracting value before the network had a chance to compound.

## The trade-off we made

In late January 2026, we deliberately pulled back monetisation. The impact was immediate - February saw an abnormally large spike in growth, engagement, and retention as the friction was removed. The product got stickier, the social graph grew faster, and the flywheel started spinning properly.

This was the right call. We chose network effects over near-term revenue, and the 10x MAU growth since validates that decision.

## The revenue is there when we want it

The monetisation infrastructure is built and tested. We're not guessing whether users will pay - we've already proven it. The question is timing, not capability.

Our plan is to reintroduce monetisation thoughtfully over the coming months, layered on top of a much larger and stickier user base. The unit economics will be significantly better at scale because:
- Larger network = stronger retention = higher LTV
- More organic growth = lower CAC
- Label partnerships create B2B revenue streams that don't require user paywalls at all

## Revenue streams

**Today (proven):**
- In-app subscriptions (weekly $6.99, monthly $9.99, quarterly $19.99)
- Premium features (profile viewers, advanced filters)

**Near-term (6-12 months):**
- Label-funded artist activations and pre-releases
- Digital Vinyl sales (download revenue that counts toward chart positioning)
- Live event ticketing
- Branded content and advertising

**Medium-term (12-18 months):**
- Label marketing spend routed through Equals as a fan acquisition channel
- Social streaming revenue share
- Premium creator tools for artists building communities on Equals

## The bigger picture

We're not building a subscription app. We're building the social layer for the music industry. The revenue model follows the network: as we own more of the fan relationship, every label, artist, and brand in the industry wants access to our platform. That's a fundamentally different (and bigger) business than selling $9.99/month subscriptions.
